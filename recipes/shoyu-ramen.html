Shoyu Ramen

Grab the perfect amount of noodles and a scoop of this umami savory broth and you will be traveling to Tokyo in no time!

Ingredients:

  • Fresh ramen noodles
  • Sesame oil
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Soy sauce
  • Cooking sake
  • Dashi
  • Mirin
  • Menma
  • Dry nori sheets
  • Chopped scallion
  • Ground white pepper

Steps:

  1. Gather all of your kitchen tools and ingredients.
  2. Next, add my broth flavoring elements: soy sauce, sake, dashi, etc. Once it’s boiling, lower the heat, cover and simmer for about 15 minutes or so.
  3. In a separate pot, boil your noodles according to the instructions on the package—but make sure to reserve a bit of your noodle water. Don’t throw all of it out! We’re going to add a cup of the noodle water to our shoyu soup base. Why? The noodle water is going to cut our concentrated soup base down to a perfect strength. Added bonus, it’s already piping hot and carrying some of the essence of the alkaline ramen noodles we just cooked.
  4. Now, if you like your noodles on the al dente side, pull them off the heat a minute before you think they’ll be perfectly cooked. Much like spaghetti, they’ll keep cooking a bit after being taken out of the boiling water. Plus, we’re putting them back into hot soup before serving.
  5. Now, divide your noodles into two bowls, add your soup and toppings and slurp away on your delicious homemade shoyu ramen!
